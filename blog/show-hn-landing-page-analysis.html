🔍What The Average Landing Page Looks Like

After analyzing 20 pages, here are the patterns that emerged. These are the choke points most indie hackers miss:

🔥 90% missing Content-Security-Policy

Nearly every page lacked a CSP header. This is the single most common security gap — and it's a one-line fix.

📱 65% had mobile rendering issues

Fixed-width containers, tiny touch targets, and unresponsive layouts. Most of these were built on a Mac and never tested on mobile.

📐 70% used magic numbers

Hardcoded pixel values, breakpoints, and timestamps embedded directly in CSS/JS instead of variables or constants.

🧠 45% had weak CTAs

"Click here" or buried signup buttons instead of clear, actionable primary CTAs. The top scoring pages had exactly one primary action per viewport.

⚡ 40% lacked loading states

No skeleton screens, no progress indicators, no feedback when content was loading. Users were left staring at blank space.

🏷️ 55% had no social proof

Testimonials, usage stats, or social proof elements were missing from most pages. The top 5 scorers all had some form of social validation.

📈Does Landing Page Quality Predict HN Success?

I compared each site's PageRX score against its HN upvotes correlation coefficient.

r = 0.12 — There is almost zero correlation between landing page quality and HN upvotes.

The page with the highest HN score (Homebrew 6.0.0 at ▲1,477) scored 84/100 — solid but not top. The highest-scoring page (92/100) had only 36 upvotes.

What this means: HN upvotes primarily reflect the idea, not the implementation. A revolutionary concept on a mediocre page will still trend. But a great page with a confused value prop will go nowhere.

The real insight: The 80-90 score pages (machine0, cc.dev, extend.ai) all had one thing in common — their value proposition was immediately clear within 3 seconds. That's what the Business Viability perspective catches.

🔒The #1 Silent Killer: Security

The Security perspective flagged the most issues across all pages. The most common findings:

  • No Content-Security-Policy meta tag — present on 18 out of 20 pages
  • Inline event handlers (onerror, onclick) — XSS vectors on 40% of pages
  • innerHTML usage — DOM-based XSS opportunities on 30%
  • Client-side storage without encryption check — 25% stored data in localStorage

The irony? These are the easiest issues to fix. A single CSP meta tag takes 10 seconds. Moving inline handlers to addEventListener takes 5 minutes. But most developers skip it entirely.

🏆What The Top 5 Did Right

The highest scorers (versowriter.app, mimicscribe.app, alexandrefrancois.org, traceapp.info, machine0.io) all shared these traits:

  • Single, clear value prop above the fold — No "everything app" claims. Each had one sentence that explained exactly what it does.
  • Clean, semantic HTML — Proper heading hierarchy, alt text on images, semantic elements (nav, main, section).
  • Mobile-responsive from the start — Not retrofitted. Font sizes, touch targets, and layouts all scaled properly.
  • No unnecessary JavaScript — Minimal dependencies, no bloated frameworks for a simple landing page.
  • Fast load times — Under 2 seconds to first contentful paint across all five.

The single biggest differentiator? Clarity of purpose. Every top scorer communicated what the product does in under 3 seconds. That's not a CSS issue — it's a positioning issue, and the AI caught it every time.
