What High-Scoring Pages Do Differently

247| 248|

We compared the top 10% of pages (scores 80+) against the bottom 50%. The differences were stark:

249| 250|

1. Crystal-clear value proposition

251|

Top pages stated what they did in under 5 words in the headline. Low-scoring pages needed 3+ paragraphs to explain themselves. If someone can't understand your product in the time it takes to blink, you're losing them.

252| 253|

2. Above-fold CTA with urgency

254|

Every top-scoring page had a visible, clickable CTA without scrolling. Most used action-oriented language ("Start free trial," "Get early access") rather than generic buttons ("Learn more," "Submit").

255| 256|

3. Social proof with numbers

257|

High performers used specific, quantified social proof. Not "Trusted by thousands" but "Serving 10,000+ companies in 50 countries." Specificity builds trust.

258| 259|

4. Pricing transparency

260|

Pages that showed pricing early scored 18% higher on conversion metrics. Hiding pricing behind "Contact Sales" forms is a conversion killer unless you're selling enterprise deals above $10k.

261| 262|

5. Mobile optimization (done right)

263|

Top pages didn't just scale down — they reorganized. Simplified navigation, larger touch targets, and optimized CTA placement for thumb zones. Desktop visitors still converted 2.1x more, but mobile was closing the gap.

Page Speed vs Conversion: What the Data Actually Says

266| 267|

Conventional CRO wisdom says every second of load time costs you 7% of conversions. Our data tells a more nuanced story:

268| 269|
    270|
  • Pages loading under 2 seconds had slightly higher average conversion scores — but the difference was only 4 points
    • 271|
  • Pages loading over 5 seconds still scored well if their content and UX were strong
    • 272|
  • Content quality mattered 3x more than load speed in predicting overall score
    • 273|
274| 275|

This doesn't mean speed doesn't matter — Google uses it as a ranking factor, and users do notice slow pages. But if you're choosing between spending a week optimizing images and spending a day writing a clearer headline, do the headline first.

Methodology

341|

All 100 pages were analyzed using PageRX's 6-dimension scoring system. The analysis combines deterministic HTML structure checks (meta tags, headers, forms, CTAs) with heuristic pattern matching (trust signals, social proof, pricing visibility) and AI-powered review for business viability assessment. Scores are computed server-side using severity-weighted penalties. No human curation was applied to the scores — every number is a direct output of the analysis engine.

342|

Pages were selected from Show HN submissions, Product Hunt launches, Y Combinator startups, and direct submissions. The sample skews toward indie and early-stage products, which may mean the benchmarks are more generous than enterprise averages.
